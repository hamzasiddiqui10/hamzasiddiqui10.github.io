About Me

I graduated with a degree in Economics and was initially on an academic path — I enjoyed the field’s mathematical rigor. After graduating I moved into the Data Science industry and that’s where I met programming. I quickly picked up R and SQL and, through focused self-study (MOOCs, tutorials, and hands-on experiments), expanded my skills into machine learning and software practices.

Over time I’ve built practical experience designing ML solutions, constructing reproducible pipelines, and applying statistical thinking to real problems. My day-to-day toolkit includes Python, R and SQL, and I keep up with developments in machine learning so I can apply the right tools to deliver value.

I’m eager to keep learning and to grow deeper expertise in machine learning.
